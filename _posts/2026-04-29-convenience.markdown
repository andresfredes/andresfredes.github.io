### And other horrors of the modern age
<!--more-->

**Convenience is making the world a worse place.**

Now, while I don't think this is actually true, I DO think it gets us thinking in the right direction. There are ills in the world that seem to strongly correlate with convenience: algorithms guiding media consumption, controlling access to information and, at the extreme, shaping world-view; privacy violations that are inherent in daily-use products and services; and a reduction in quality and ownership of those very same products and services. So, what is actually happening here?

**Convenience is a trap.**

This gets us a little closer. It makes sense that anything that can make our lives a little easier would be a good thing. There are two big traps that I see associated with convenience that I think of as the *Goals* and the *Tradeoffs*.

What is the *Goal* of the convenience? That is, what does \<insert-your-convenient-product-or-service-here\> actually make more convenient? As an example, YouTube makes it really easy to watch an endless amount of videos about anything I could be interested in. Do I want to be watching an endless amount of video content? 

What are the *Tradeoffs* being made for this convenience? The common tradeoffs are in the form of privacy, but can include security, quality of life and more else, besides. For example, it is easier than ever to communicate with anyone in the world. It is also a sad fact that the majority of that communication is not as private as it looks, with each message being seen, stored and used for all manner of practices by various third parties.

**Convenience is an exploitable weakness in people.**

I think this gets us closer again. We have a very limited ability to pay attention to the immense amount of information that the world throws at us each day, so we take short cuts and use heuristics as a natural response to try and manage. These behaviours are predictable, and in our high-tech world, very exploitable. Consider these two possible situations when signing up to a fitness tracker:
"Warning! By signing up, we will use every piece of information we can record or otherwise infer about your personal life, habits and preferences, and then sell those to people who want to manipulate you. Do you agree?"
VS
"Do you accept these terms of service?" Followed by pages of incomprehensible legal jargon.

**Offering convenience is financially lucrative and easily exploitable.**

If something can make companies money then it makes financial sense for them to do so. Some of the constraints on this are privacy regulations and ethical business practices, both of which are in short supply.

**What can we do?**

When making a purchase or signing up for something new, stop and think: Do you know what *Tradeoffs* you are making? Does this company need your private information? Will they be treating your information securely, respectfully and ethically? What data/information will they even be taking that might not be obvious? This process is difficult, because there is money to be made from it being difficult. This process can also be confronting, so be easy on yourself and make changes a little at a time.

Most importantly: regularly assessing what we truly want in our lives makes this whole situation easier to manage. If something is convenient AND it takes you toward your *Goals* in life, that is great! How can you make your life goals a bit more convenient and the things you don't want/need a bit less so?

There is a lot more to discuss here, but I will leave it there for now. Good luck!
