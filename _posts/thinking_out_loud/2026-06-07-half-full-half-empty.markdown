### Does it depend on the drink?
<!--more-->

When making coffee at the Barista Championships, a contestant will be assessed by a variety of judges, each with their own scorecards. In the case of Technical Judges, their scorecard presents a list of skills and processes that the competitor will perform, with a checkbox for success at that process or 0 to 6 score on how well it was done. For example, during the step where the competitor is 'dosing' grounds from the grinder into the filter basket, any stray grounds that escape and fall onto the workspace are waste that reduce the score for that step. 

In this case, the starting condition was a perfectly clean workstation and so any rogue coffee grounds or milk residue are imperfections which get penalised.

In contrast, when a baby is learning new skills like walking or talking, each small bit of progress is a marvellous feat. Parents understandably feel a sense of pride and wonder at each newly learned skill because the starting condition was a complete inability to do so.

With those situations in mind, what is the correct framing for AI?

Lately, I have witnessed conversations about AI capabilities unfold in a way that sounds to my ears like a proud new parent arguing with a technical judge. Not only are they using a different method of assessing things, the underlying framing is different.
